Overview

GroupMe is a mobile group messaging app owned by Microsoft. It was launched in May 2010 by the private company GroupMe. In 2013, GroupMe had over 12 million registered users.

User Needs and Preferences

The Problem

While GroupMe works for well for cross-platform communications and scheduling events, there are some frustrations with certain components of the system. The following "pain points" were created as a response to data collected from users, and serve as a response to three of the most common user complaints.

Pain Point 1: No ability to sort messages

GroupMe Messages are always displayed in order of recency. Unlike in many apps, there is no way to keep favorites at the top, or hide muted groups. App users want to feel like they are in control, and frequently become frustrated when their choices are restricted. Giving users options is essential for companies that want to stay relevant. Where there are no options there is generally no business.

Pain Point 2: Group chat logistics

Adding new people to a group is overly complicated and requires sifting through several menus. Since chats are by far the most popular feature in GroupMe, these should be the easiest to navigate. It makes sense that some of the secondary features are harder to reach. However, work could be done to reduce the time it takes to add someone to a group. Group chats are supposed to be what GroupMe excels at, and yet they continue to be far more complicated than they should.

Pain Point 3: App feels outdated

The app hasn't kept up with recent design trends. Sure, it's generally pretty user friendly, but it fails to "wow" users with its visual appearance. Part of that has to do with the fact that it's rather blocky - lots of hardline edges and list items laid out with hardly any interesting formatting. It feels a little bit like a generic, run-of-the-mill messaging app, and not a flagship, industry-standard communication tool.

Design Question: How might we improve the experience of GroupMe users as they navigate and manage their conversations?

The Solution

By reducing the number of steps to add a new person to a group, we can simpify the user experience and keep users more likely to remain loyal to GroupMe. In the process, we will solve pain point #2 by removing a layer of navigation. I chose to provide a solution for this particular pain point becuase it would make the biggest difference to users with the lowest learning curve. A new interface, as provided by fixing pain points 1 or 3, would undoubtedly benefit users, but would require them to completely relearn the system.

Conclusion

GroupMe is a powerful app with a lot of functionality in a compact space. By refining the add members feature, we are able to further simplify and streamline the user experience. In the future, I would love to have more time dedicated to research, as there is a lot to learn from other users. I feel that a lot more that could be covered, given enough time and resources.
